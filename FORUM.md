# Community Forum & Founders Group

This document describes the community discussion spaces for **Immortal Bot** — including the **GitHub Discussions board** and the **Founding Cohort** group.

---

## Purpose

The community forum serves four overlapping goals:

1. **Governance** — Deliberate on project direction, lazy-consensus proposals, and policy changes in an open, asynchronous space.
2. **Founding Cohort onboarding** — Welcome and orient the first wave of contributors who are shaping the project's foundations.
3. **Patent-free science advocacy** — Share news, research, and arguments that advance the case for open, patent-free scientific collaboration.
4. **Global reach** — Build a geographically and culturally diverse contributor base; surface barriers to participation and work to lower them.

---

## Where to discuss

| Space | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **GitHub Discussions** (this repo) | Structured, threaded conversations; governance proposals; onboarding Q&A |
| **GitHub Issues** | Concrete bug reports and actionable feature requests |
| **Pull Requests** | Code and doc review |

> GitHub Discussions can be enabled by a repository maintainer via **Settings → Features → Discussions**.  
> Discussion templates live in [`.github/DISCUSSION_TEMPLATE/`](.github/DISCUSSION_TEMPLATE/).

## Founding Cohort

The **Founding Cohort** is the initial group of contributors who join before the project's first stable release. Members of the Founding Cohort:

- Are listed (with consent) in [`CITATION.cff`](CITATION.cff) under `authors`.
- Help set norms, vote on governance proposals, and mentor future contributors.
- Agree to the [OSNA-style non-assert pledge](ONBOARDING.md#contribution-gate-osna-style-non-assert-pledge-required).

### How to join the Founding Cohort

1. Read [`ONBOARDING.md`](ONBOARDING.md) and the [`LICENSE`](LICENSE).
2. Introduce yourself in a **Founders Group** Discussion thread.
3. Open or contribute to at least one Issue or Pull Request.
4. A maintainer will add you to `CITATION.cff` as an author (with your consent).

There is no application form. Participation is the qualification.

## Governance discussions

Governance discussions follow the same lazy-consensus model as Issues (see [`GOVERNANCE.md`](GOVERNANCE.md)):

- **Routine timebox**: 72 hours — no objections → proposal passes.
- **Governance / licensing / publication timebox**: 7 days.

To raise a governance topic:
1. Open a new Discussion in the **Governance** category.
2. State the proposal, rationale, and timebox clearly.
3. If the discussion matures into a concrete change, convert it to an Issue using the [Proposal template](.github/ISSUE_TEMPLATE/proposal.md).

## Patent-free science advocacy

This project is explicitly aligned with the principles of the [Zenodo Dataset (DOI 10.5281/zenodo.17053441)](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.17053441) and the *Patent-Free Science in the Janus Era* framework.

Use the **Patent-Free Science** Discussion category to:
- Share peer-reviewed papers, preprints, or data sets released under open/patent-free terms.
- Discuss policy developments (national or international) that affect open science.
- Coordinate defensive publication efforts (see [Issue #1](https://github.com/BrewtaniusAI/immortal-bot/issues/1)).

## Global reach

To lower barriers for international contributors:
- Discussions and Issues may be opened in any language; maintainers will respond in English and, where possible, in the original language.
- If you notice a barrier to participation (time zone coverage, language, tooling access), open a **Global Reach** Discussion thread.
- The project welcomes mirror/translation volunteers.

## Code of conduct

All community spaces are governed by the project's commitment to open, respectful collaboration as described in [`SOUL.md`](SOUL.md). Violations may be reported by opening a **private** Issue or contacting a maintainer directly.
